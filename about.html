Hi, my name is Daniel Hartgrove. Welcome to my website.

Since I began programming at age 11 making simple games and animations in Scratch I have developed a keen interest in the production of video games and system applications. Throughout secondary school, I programmed for fun in Scratch, Python and Visual Basic and achieved high grades in all of my related school work. By Sixth Form, I had a working knowledge of VB, Java and Python under my belt and was able to produce and design an application to simulate natural selection designed as a teaching tool for biology classes. This was my first experience with simulation and computational autonomy. During this project, I discovered a love for designing and developing user interfaces, starting with paper and TKinter. Now, as a student at the University of Nottingham, I am specialising in Software Development modules with a keen interest in UI/UX design. It was here that I picked up an interest in Web design.

As a side hobby throughout my time at school, I found graphic design work to be exciting and interesting to learn about. I have since developed my love of Graphic Design into a side project to show off my skills creatively, which can be found here. In the future, I hope to continue to merge my love of design and computing to publish original yet inspired content in the form of web design, software development and graphics.
